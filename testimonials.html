CANADIANS FIGHTING FOR DIGITAL RIGHTS

Their Stories Matter

Bill C-34 threatens the digital freedom of millions of Canadian youth and anonymity of everyone. Here's what people across the country are saying about the proposed ban on social media.

"Where do they draw the line? Is discord a social media(probably)? How about roblox? What about steam? Every platform is trying to be a social media, look at Spotify. Is there a way to protect kids without taking away everyone’s privacy?"

— NuclearHateLizard Reddit

" the kids who do bypass the filter [will] be in a space full of adults and without their peers. That's a can of worms on its own. "

— AileStrike Reddit

"Kids are always going to find their way onto the internet, and it's only going to cause people to be more sneaky about it.... If you're going to [limit social media], I think it should be under 16 with restrictions, not entirely banned."

— Xavier Carr, High school student (Source: CBC Jessica Wong) Toronto

"We aren't going to ban our way out of the problems caused by these platforms. It isn't like cigarettes or booze where you can expect a reliable gatekeeper at least some of the time. "

— Hoss-Bonaventure_ZERO Reddit

"This is nothing but a blatant attempt to force people to link their real IDs to their online identity (which is anon for good reason). If nothing else, this is identity fraud extravaganza for any criminal group out there given security is by no means consistent across platforms but now ALL platforms will be required to keep your information. "

— swpz01 Reddit

"The biggest question I have is how? I see the liberal talking points. They talk about what they aim to achieve and what the goal is. I don’t think I’ve heard a single proposition on how it will be done or enforced. It’s like saying “we’re going to the moon today” but we haven’t even built the space ship yet..."

— LurkerGarry Prince Edward Island
